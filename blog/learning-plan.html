Learning Plan v1.1 Updates

Foundations is over and it is time to review my learning plan in light of new insights.

Goals

Firstly, lets make a more direct statement on my long-term goals. My ultimate goal is to use a career in software or web development for effective altruism. I want to use the flexibility that comes with having an in-demand skill set to create time for managing investments and maximising the amount of money I can donate to effective charities. With that in mind, I don't have a particular career pathway in mind. What I end up doing is going to be driven by what's most in demand and accessible to me at that time.

Strengths and Limitations

Before I completed foundations I thought that I never doubt my technical abilities. I'm going to moderate that claim and say that I rarely do, and when I do, it's not for an extended period of time. My confident belief that I will see how all the pieces fit together always comes back quickly if it ever wavers.

My biggest limitations will be confidence in non-technical matters. Presenting ideas and negotiating in a confident, engaging manner rather than simply acquiescing or avoiding the process entirely. Building the kinds of projects that are going to impress employers and land me a job is going to depend on building productive relationships in Bootcamp. That's going to require putting myself out there every day and persevering through negative feedback.

I would like to develop my ability to give presentations, to give and receive feedback, and generally engage with other people gracefully.

Expectations

From the facilitators, if they can be anywhere as available and engaged as they are in foundations, that would be awesome.

From myself, that I show up every day on time as engaged as I reasonably can be. I meal prep, focus on essential household tasks and phone a friend for help with any time-consuming miscellaneous tasks.

I find that I bounce back quickly when things get overwhelming, so if things get to be too much I will take some kind of time-out. Perhaps go for a walk, run or hike, grab coffee with a friend, or just spend some time alone with a book. Helps clear away the tunnel vision and allow me to gain a more holistic perspective. If I'm spending time I don't have stuck on technical issues I will ask for help from peers, and if that doesn't get me unstuck I will request a tech-in.

Learning Plan v1.0

My long-term goal is to have the career and lifestyle flexibility that comes with being a skilled software developer.

I have a lot of patience and focus which allows me to dig into the nitty gritty even when it isn't a very pleasurable experience. I never doubt my technical abilities. Even if I feel completely lost I always know it is simply a matter of time before I see how all the pieces fit. On the other hand, when the challenge is non-technical my confidence can waver and I can begin to lose focus. I would like to see my ability to collaborate and present ideas to others improve, and I would like to gain further confidence in negotiating with others. I also spent so long touching grass with the working class at New World that I need to reacclimate to a corporate environment.

I plan to maintain resilience by taking quality breaks from study, doing things I value or enjoy. Running, hiking and weightlifting, lunch or dinner breaks with friends and family, progressing important personal projects where I can. Many times when I have felt overwhelmed or I have begun to lose sight of the greater purpose of my efforts, going for a hike has allowed me to regain perspective, see the challenges ahead of me clearly and restore my resilience. I have a couple of close friends I am frequently in contact with that I can reach out to for support if things get tough. Family are also close by but eww family.

I expect facilitators to be available to respond to queries about the course and expectations, particularly during bootcamp. I expect a degree of professionalism, empathy and charitability from the facilitation team.

I do not anticipate any commitments getting in the way of my studies.
