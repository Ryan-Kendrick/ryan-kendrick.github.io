Emotional Intelligence

Emotional intelligence is your ability to understand your own emotions, and the emotions of other people. Beyond simply recognising the presence of particular emotions, emotional intelligence concerns recognising the origins of particular emotions and the influence those emotions have on thoughts and actions. It is a fundamental ability for success in the workplace, and life in general.

Knowledge of Self

Managing your emotions in an emotionally intelligent manner means not acting impulsively. It could involve taking a time-out when a stressful situation emerges, so that you can compose yourself and respond in a more careful manner. An intentional, considered response is more likely to result in actions that will not be looked back upon with regret.

Knowledge of Others

Understanding the perspective of others is a key component of emotional intelligence. It involves understanding another person's point of view, often through the practice of empathy. An emotionally intelligent person is able to understand not only what another person is feeling, but why they came to that feeling and what is needed to change that feeling, especially if that feeling is leading to conflict. A great demonstration of someones emotional intelligence is their ability to describe to another person what their point of view is, without that person raising an objection to the way their point of view is being characterised.

Difference from IQ

In summary, traditional IQ measures focus more on technical aspects of intelligence like analytical thinking and abstract thought. Emotional intelligence relates to social intelligence and the social skills that enables.
